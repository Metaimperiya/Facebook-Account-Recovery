Facebook usually sends a reason for the ban to security@facebookmail.com or in the notification tab. Check your Spam folder — sometimes emails end up there. If there's no email, the ban was automatic without explanation.

💡 Tip: Add Facebook email to your white list to avoid missing important messages.

Fill out the Facebook Appeal Form with your real data. Attach your ID if requested. Be concise and honest — algorithms don't read long texts. Say: "My account was blocked by mistake, I didn't violate rules."

  • Use the real name from your account
  • Attach a photo of your passport or ID if requested
  • Submit the appeal multiple times from different browsers
⚠️ If the "Submit" button doesn't work — try a different browser or clear your cache.

If you have a Business Manager — contact support from there. They respond faster. Business Manager gives access to live chat support, unlike regular users.

  • Go to Business Manager → Help → Contact Support
  • Choose chat or email
  • Describe the problem and add your account ID

Try these emails:

  • disabled@facebook.com
  • appeals@facebook.com
  • support@facebook.com

Include: your name, account email, page ID and briefly describe the situation. Attach a photo of your ID if available.

⚠️ The chance of a reply is very low, but it's worth trying. Sometimes automated responses arrive after 2-3 weeks.

⚠️ Nobody can unlock Facebook for $50.

If "specialists" promise to unlock your account for money — they are scammers. They will take your money and disappear, or worse — block you again.

  • Never share your passwords with anyone
  • Don't click on suspicious links
  • Remember: Facebook support is always free

📌 In any case — prepare a new account

Reality is: even if you submit an appeal and contact support — the chance of unblocking is minimal. Algorithms don't listen, support doesn't respond, there are no real people inside Facebook.

Therefore, the only working solution is to create a new account and start from scratch. Yes, it hurts. Yes, it's unfair. But that's reality.

💡 Tip: create an account on a business email, enable two-factor authentication, and don't violate the rules. This will reduce the risk of being banned.

Remember: if you run a business — don't keep everything in one account. Create multiple profiles, use Business Manager, and make backups of your client base.
